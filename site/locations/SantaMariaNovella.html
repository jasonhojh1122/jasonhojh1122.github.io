Artworks

Sort by:

Crucifix

Giotto di Bondone Tempera on panel c. 1290–1300

The earliest known artwork by Giotto. Revolutionary in its naturalistic depiction of Christ's suffering body, departing from the rigid Byzantine tradition.

Rose Window

Andrea da Firenze Stained glass c. 1365

Stained glass rose window imported from France.

Chapter House Fresco

Andrea da Firenze Fresco c. 1365–1367

Fresco in the Chapter House (Spanish Chapel) at Santa Maria Novella, depicting the "Triumph of the Church."

Trinity

Masaccio Fresco c. 1427

A revolutionary work demonstrating Brunelleschi's principles of linear perspective. One of the first monumental Renaissance paintings to utilize linear perspective. The donors are shown kneeling on either side of the holy scene.

Story of Noah

Paolo Uccello Fresco c. 1447

Fresco depicting scenes from the Story of Noah in the Chiostro Verde (Green Cloister), demonstrating Uccello's fascination with perspective.

Santa Maria Novella Facade

Alberti Architecture (marble) 1456–1470

The facade of Santa Maria Novella, designed by Alberti. The only green and white marble structure in Florence.

Tornabuoni Chapel Frescoes

Domenico Ghirlandaio Fresco and stained glass 1485–1490

Series of frescoes and stained glass in the Tornabuoni Chapel. This was a work of Ghirlandaio's whole shop, including a 13-year-old apprentice named Michelangelo.
