Baptistery Ceiling Mosaic

Coppo di Marcovaldo Mosaic c. 1270–1300

Part of the ceiling of the Baptistery of Florence, representing the Italo-Byzantine style of the Duecento.

South Door of Florence Baptistery

Andrea Pisano Bronze 1330–1336

The earliest of the three sets of bronze doors on the Florence Baptistery.

North Door of Florence Baptistery

Lorenzo Ghiberti Bronze 1403–1424

The second set of bronze doors for the Florence Baptistery, created after Ghiberti won the famous competition against Brunelleschi.

Gates of Paradise

Lorenzo Ghiberti Gilded bronze 1425–1452

The third and most famous set of doors for the Florence Baptistery. Contains the first sensuous female nudes of the Renaissance. Named "Gates of Paradise" by Michelangelo.
