Pala d'Oro Self-researched

Gold, enamel, precious gems 10th-14th century (created in phases)

The high altar retable of the Basilica, universally recognized as one of the most refined works of Byzantine enamel. Adorned with 1,927 precious gems including 526 pearls, 330 garnets, 320 emeralds, 255 sapphires, and 75 rubies. The jewel-encrusted altarpiece depicts scenes from the life of Christ and took 366 years to complete.

Horses of Saint Mark Self-researched

Bronze with gold gilding Antiquity (possibly 4th century BC or 2nd century AD)

A group of four bronze horses that originally formed part of a quadriga monument. Brought to Venice from Constantinople after the Fourth Crusade in 1204. The originals are now in the basilica museum, with replicas on the facade. Also known as the Triumphal Quadriga.

Byzantine Mosaics of San Marco Self-researched

Gold and glass mosaic 11th-13th century (and later)

The basilica features 8,000 square meters of mosaics illuminating the interior. The oldest surviving mosaic, depicting Saint Alipio, dates to 1260 and shows the translation of Saint Mark's relics from Alexandria, with merchants hiding the body beneath pork to avoid Muslim inspection.
