Temple of Vespasian and Titus Self-researched

Wikipedia
  • Location: Roman Forum, Rome
  • Date: 79-87 AD

Located at the western end of the Roman Forum between the Temple of Concordia and the Temple of Saturn. Dedicated to the deified Vespasian, it was begun by Titus after Vespasian's death and completed by Domitian. Three elegant Corinthian columns still stand, supporting a section of the ornate entablature with a frieze depicting sacrificial instruments.
