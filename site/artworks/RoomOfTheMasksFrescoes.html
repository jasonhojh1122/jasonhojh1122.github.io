Room of the Masks Frescoes Self-researched

  • Location: Palatine Hill, Rome (House of Augustus)
  • Medium: Fresco (Second Style Roman wall painting)
  • Date: c. 30 BC

Perspective architectural paintings and theatrical masks in the western rooms of the House of Augustus, representing the Second Style of Roman wall painting. The illusionistic architectural scenes create the impression of views through walls into elaborate stage sets. These frescoes are among the finest surviving examples of Second Style painting, demonstrating the Roman mastery of perspective and trompe-l'oeil techniques.
