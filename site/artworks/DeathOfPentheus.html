Death of Pentheus Self-researched

Wikipedia
  • Location: Pompeii, House of the Vettii (VI.15.1), Southern Triclinium
  • Medium: Fresco
  • Date: c. 62-70 CE (Fourth Style)

A powerful Fourth Style fresco depicting the violent climax of the Pentheus myth. The scene shows Pentheus being discovered and attacked by the maenads (female followers of Dionysus).

Key elements:

  • Center: A maenad holds a stone above her head, about to hurl it at Pentheus
  • Figures: Pentheus's mother Agave and aunts Ino and AutonoÃ« participate in the killing
  • Upper corners: Two Erinyes (Furies) appear, with one raising a whip
  • Composition: Shows the moment of recognition and initial attack

The fresco serves as a moral warning about the consequences of defying the gods. It is one of twelve surviving mythological panels in the House of the Vettii, believed to be copied from Greek models.

The House of the Vettii was owned by two freedmen brothers and features some of the finest Fourth Style frescoes in Pompeii.
