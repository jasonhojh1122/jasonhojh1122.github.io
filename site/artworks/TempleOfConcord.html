Temple of Concord Self-researched

Wikipedia
  • Location: Roman Forum, Rome
  • Date: 367 BC (traditional); rebuilt 121 BC and again 7 BC-10 AD by Tiberius

A series of temples dedicated to the Roman goddess Concordia, located at the western end of the Roman Forum beneath the Capitoline Hill. The final version rebuilt by Tiberius housed an important collection of Greek art. Only the platform and fragments remain today, but the temple's unusual wider-than-deep floor plan is visible.
