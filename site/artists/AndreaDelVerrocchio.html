Italian Art

Andrea del Verrocchio

Wikipedia

Born: c. 1435, Florence Died: 1488, Venice

Italian sculptor, painter and goldsmith who was a master of a workshop in Florence. He was the teacher of Leonardo da Vinci, with whom he painted the Baptism of Christ.

The Baptism of Christ

Oil and tempera on panel c. 1472–1475

Painted primarily by Verrocchio, but the leftmost angel, the water and rocks behind the angel are attributed to his young apprentice [Leonardo da Vinci](../artists/LeonardoDaVinci.md).

David

Bronze c. 1473–1475

Bronze sculpture depicting David as a young man, with leather cloth and a smiling expression instead of the uncertain expression seen in earlier depictions.

Portrait of a Lady with Flowers

Marble c. 1475–1480

First three-quarter-length sculpted portrait since antiquity. A landmark in Renaissance portraiture.

Equestrian Statue of Bartolomeo Colleoni

Bronze 1480–1488
