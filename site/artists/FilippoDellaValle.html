Filippo della Valle Self-researched

Wikipedia

Born: 1698, Florence Died: 1768, Rome Source: self-research

Italian late-Baroque or early Neoclassic sculptor, active mostly in Rome. He contributed the allegorical statues of Health and Abundance to the Trevi Fountain, positioned in niches flanking the central figure of Oceanus. He also created the Annunciation altarpiece in the Ludovisi Chapel at Sant'Ignazio.

Allegory of Health (Salubrity) Self-researched

Marble sculpture c. 1751-1762

Positioned in the right niche flanking Oceanus, this female figure holds a cup from which a serpent drinks, the traditional symbol of health. She represents the healing and life-giving properties of fresh water.

Allegory of Abundance Self-researched

Marble sculpture c. 1751-1762

One of two allegorical statues flanking the central niche, positioned in the left niche. The female figure holds a cornucopia overflowing with fruits, symbolizing the abundance that water brings to the land.

Sculpture 18th century

The altarpiece in the Ludovisi Chapel dedicated to the Annunciation. The chapel houses the remains of Saint John Berchmans, a Belgian Jesuit scholastic who died while studying at the Roman College.
