Donatello

Wikipedia

Born: c. 1386, Florence, Republic of Florence Died: December 13, 1466, Florence

Born Donato di Niccolò di Betto Bardi, Donatello was an Italian sculptor of the Renaissance period. He studied classical sculpture and developed an Early Renaissance style. Belonged to the Arte di Pietra e Legname, the guild of workers in stone and wood. His periods in Rome, Padua, and Siena introduced his techniques to other parts of Italy.

Marble David

Marble 1408-1416

First large-scale nude sculpture since antiquity.

St. Mark

Marble 1411–1413

Commissioned for the Arte dei Linaioli e Rigattiri (linen weavers and peddlers). Cloth and drapery are more natural and realistic than Ghiberti's St. John the Baptist.

St. George and the Dragon

Marble relief 1416–1417

Predella relief originally below the St. George statue. Features a new approach that focuses on light and shadows, letting the figure blend into the background.

St. George

Marble 1416–1417

Originally part of the sculptural program at Orsanmichele, now in the Bargello.

Cantoria (Donatello)

Marble 1433–1439

Singing gallery (cantoria) originally made for Florence Cathedral. Features a dynamic frieze of dancing putti.

Bronze David

Bronze c. 1440s

First large-scale nude sculpture since antiquity. The first unsupported standing work of bronze cast during the Renaissance, and the first freestanding nude male sculpture made since antiquity. The pose is derived from contrapposto, with the left knee coming forward against the cloth. David is depicted with an enigmatic smile, posed with his foot on Goliath's severed head.

Atys-Amorino

Bronze c. 1440

Bronze sculpture combining attributes of Eros and Attis.

Penitent Magdalene

Wood (polychrome) c. 1453–1455

Late work by Donatello depicting Mary Magdalene as an emaciated penitent, demonstrating his expressive late style.

Judith and Holofernes

Bronze c. 1457–1464

Bronze sculpture by Donatello depicting the climactic moment of the biblical narrative.
