Italian Art

Masaccio

Wikipedia

Born: December 21, 1401, San Giovanni Valdarno, Republic of Florence Died: 1428, Rome

Born Tommaso di Ser Giovanni di Simone, Masaccio was the first great Italian painter of the Quattrocento period. According to Vasari, he was the best painter of his generation because of his skill at imitating nature, recreating lifelike figures and movements as well as a convincing sense of three-dimensionality.

Artworks

Trinity

Fresco c. 1427

A revolutionary work demonstrating Brunelleschi's principles of linear perspective. One of the first monumental Renaissance paintings to utilize linear perspective. The donors are shown kneeling on either side of the holy scene.
