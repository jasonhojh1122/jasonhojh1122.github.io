Italian Art

Luca della Robbia

Wikipedia

Born: c. 1400, Florence, Republic of Florence Died: February 20, 1482, Florence

Italian Renaissance sculptor noted for his colorful, tin-glazed terracotta statuary, a technique that he invented and passed on to his nephew Andrea della Robbia. Although a leading sculptor in stone, after developing his technique in the early 1440s he worked primarily in terracotta.

Marble 1431–1438

A marble singing gallery (cantoria) originally made for Florence Cathedral. The relief panels depict joyful children and youths singing, dancing, and playing musical instruments, illustrating Psalm 150.
