Cristofano Allori Self-researched

Wikipedia

Born: 1577, Florence Died: 1621, Florence Source: self-research

An Italian painter of the late Florentine Mannerist school, son of painter Alessandro Allori and great-nephew of Bronzino. He initially trained under his father but later turned to the naturalistic style of Cigoli and Santi di Tito. He painted mostly portraits and religious subjects, and is best known for his Judith with the Head of Holofernes in the Palazzo Pitti.

Artworks

Judith with the Head of Holofernes Self-researched

Oil on canvas 1613

One of the most celebrated paintings of the Florentine Seicento, depicting Judith holding the severed head of Holofernes while her maidservant waits with a bag. Allori reportedly used his own face as the model for Holofernes, his mistress for Judith, and her mother for the maidservant, lending the painting an intensely personal subtext. Multiple versions exist, with this being the finest.
