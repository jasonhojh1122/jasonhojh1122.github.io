Born: c. 1415, Borgo Sansepolcro Died: October 12, 1492, Borgo Sansepolcro Source: self-research

Italian painter of the Early Renaissance. Piero della Francesca was among the foremost painters of the 15th century, known for his serene, geometric compositions and pioneering use of perspective. His mathematical approach to painting influenced subsequent generations of artists.

Vault Fresco Fragments (St. Michael Chapel) Self-researched

Fresco Mid-15th century

Fragments of vault frescoes in the Chapel of St. Michael, attributed to Piero della Francesca. These damaged remnants are among the few surviving traces of the artist's work in Rome, executed during the pontificate of Nicholas V or Pius II. Most of Piero's Roman frescoes were destroyed to make way for later decorations.
