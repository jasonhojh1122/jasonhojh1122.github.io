Paolo Veronese Self-researched

Wikipedia

Born: 1528, Verona Died: 1588, Venice Source: self-research

Italian Renaissance painter based in Venice, known for extremely large history paintings of religion and mythology. With Titian and Tintoretto, Veronese is one of the "great trio that dominated Venetian painting of the cinquecento." Known as a supreme colorist who developed a naturalist style influenced by Titian.

Juno Showering Gifts on Venice Self-researched

Oil on canvas (ceiling) 1553-1554

Located in the Council of Ten Room, this ceiling painting shows the goddess Juno bestowing gifts upon the personification of Venice, symbolizing the city's prosperity and divine favor.

The Feast in the House of Levi Self-researched

Oil on canvas 1573

One of the largest canvases of the 16th century (555 x 1,309 cm). Originally painted as a Last Supper for a Dominican friary, Veronese's irreverent interpretation led to an investigation by the Inquisition. Rather than make changes, he simply renamed the painting. A masterpiece of Venetian colorism.

The Rape of Europa Self-researched

Oil on canvas c. 1580

Located in the Sala dell'Anticollegio, this mythological painting depicts the abduction of Europa by Zeus in the form of a white bull. It hangs alongside Tintoretto's allegories.

Apotheosis of Venice Self-researched

Oil on canvas (ceiling) 1585

A ceiling painting in the Chamber of the Great Council celebrating Venice personified, surrounded by allegorical figures representing Peace, Justice, and Fame. A masterpiece of illusionistic ceiling painting.
