Filippo Brunelleschi

Wikipedia

Born: 1377, Florence, Republic of Florence Died: April 15, 1446, Florence

Italian architect, designer, goldsmith, and sculptor. Considered a founding father of Renaissance architecture and the first modern engineer. He developed linear perspective and in 1421 became the first person to receive a patent in the Western world.

Competition Panel (Brunelleschi)

Bronze relief 1401

Brunelleschi's competition panel for the second set of bronze doors for the Florence Baptistery. The servant in the left's pose is derived from the Spinario, a popular Roman sculpture. Each figure was cast individually.

Ospedale degli Innocenti

Architecture 1419–1445

Hospital of the Innocents, designed by Brunelleschi. Provided orphans and children with housing and education until age 18. Children were given the last name "Innocenti."

Dome of Florence Cathedral

Architecture (brick) 1420–1436

Revolutionary dome designed by Brunelleschi. Exactly half as wide as it is tall. The dome consists of an inner and outer shell.

Old Sacristy (Sagrestia Vecchia)

Architecture 1421–1428

One of the earliest Renaissance interiors. Commissioned by Giovanni di Bicci de' Medici as a new sacristy for the old church of San Lorenzo.
