Lorenzo Ghiberti

Wikipedia

Born: 1378, Florence, Republic of Florence Died: December 1, 1455, Florence

Italian Renaissance sculptor, a key figure in the Early Renaissance, best known as the creator of two sets of bronze doors of the Florence Baptistery, the later one called by Michelangelo the Gates of Paradise. Trained as a goldsmith and sculptor, he established an important workshop for sculpture in metal. New approach to sculpt eyes: cut out the line of the cornea and dot of the pupil.

Competition Panel (Ghiberti)

Bronze relief 1401

Ghiberti's winning competition panel for the second set of bronze doors for the Florence Baptistery. Only the Isaac figure was cast separately, making the whole piece lighter but stronger.

North Door of Florence Baptistery My Study Self-researched

Bronze 1403–1424

Ghiberti's first masterpiece, made before his celebrated Gates of Paradise. The doors contain scenes from the New Testament in quatrefoil frames. After restoration in 2013-2015, the original doors were displayed in the Museo dell'Opera del Duomo and replaced by a copy on the Baptistery. Created after Ghiberti won the famous competition against Brunelleschi.

St. John the Baptist

Bronze 1412–1416

Part of the sculptural program at Orsanmichele. Ghiberti developed a new approach to sculpting eyes, cutting out the line of the cornea and dot of the pupil.

Saint John the Baptist Self-researched

Bronze 1412-1416

A bronze statue commissioned by the cloth merchant's guild (Arte di Calimala). The first life-sized bronze cast in the Renaissance and the first bronze statue of its size to be cast in a single piece for several hundred years in Italy. The artist's use of unnaturalistic but elegant curves in the hair and drapery of the saint show the influence of the International Gothic style prevalent in Italy at the time the work was created. The statue marks an important technical achievement in bronze casting and helped establish Ghiberti's reputation, leading to his famous commission for the Baptistery doors.

Gates of Paradise My Study Self-researched

Gilded bronze 1425–1452

The second set of bronze doors Ghiberti created for the Baptistery, depicting Old Testament scenes in ten rectangular panels. Michelangelo reportedly said they were worthy of being the "Gates of Paradise." The original panels are now displayed in glass cases in the Museo dell'Opera del Duomo. Contains the first sensuous female nudes of the Renaissance.
