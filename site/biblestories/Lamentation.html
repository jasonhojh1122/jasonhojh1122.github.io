Lamentation (Pietà)

Wikipedia

Summary

The Lamentation (also known as Pietà when focused on Mary alone with Christ's body) depicts the mourning over Christ's body after the Crucifixion and Deposition, but before his burial. While not directly described in scripture, this scene is derived from the burial accounts in the Gospels. The Virgin Mary typically holds or sits near the body of her dead son, accompanied by other mourners such as Mary Magdalene, John the Evangelist, Joseph of Arimathea, and Nicodemus. The scene emphasizes the human sorrow and grief following Christ's death.

Biblical Reference

Book(s): All four Gospels (derived from burial accounts)

Chapters/Verses: Matthew 27:57-61, Mark 15:42-47, Luke 23:50-56, John 19:38-42

Giovanni Bellini Tempera on panel c. 1465–1470

The Foreshortened Christ (Lamentation of Christ)

Andrea Mantegna Tempera on canvas c. 1480

Famous for its dramatic use of foreshortening, showing Christ's body from the feet with the wounds in his feet prominently visible.

Lamentation over the Dead Christ Self-researched

Pietro Perugino Oil on panel 1495

A large and emotionally restrained depiction of the lamentation, showing the dead Christ mourned by the Virgin Mary, Saint John the Evangelist, Mary Magdalene, and other figures. Perugino's characteristic grace and soft, luminous coloring are evident, with a serene Umbrian landscape visible in the background. Displayed in the Sala di Apollo.

Pietà

Michelangelo Marble 1498-1499

Created when Michelangelo was only 23 years old. The sculpture was designed to be placed on or near the floor, so the viewer can see Christ's face better. It is the only work Michelangelo ever signed, reportedly after overhearing viewers attribute it to another sculptor.

Palestrina Pietà Self-researched

Marble c. 1555

A marble sculpture depicting the dead Christ supported by the Virgin Mary and another figure. Formerly attributed to Michelangelo, but now considered to have possibly been completed by someone else, such as Niccolò Menghini or Gian Lorenzo Bernini. The attribution remains controversial.

Pietà Self-researched

Titian Oil on canvas 1575-1576

One of Titian's last paintings, left incomplete at his death in 1576 and finished by Palma Giovane. Titian intended it for his own grave. A profoundly moving late work demonstrating the artist's loose, expressive brushwork in his final years.
